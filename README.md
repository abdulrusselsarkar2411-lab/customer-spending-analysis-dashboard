## 🧠 SQL Analysis, Power BI Insights & Business Understanding

This project involved end-to-end analysis of customer spending behavior to support credit card strategy decisions.

### 🔍 SQL Analysis
- Cleaned and validated data by checking for null values and duplicate customer records
- Joined customer demographic data (`dim_customers`) with transaction data (`fact_spends`) to create a unified dataset
- Created key metrics such as Total Spend, Average Spend, and Income Utilization % (spend vs income)
- Analyzed spending patterns across categories, cities, payment types, and customer demographics
- Segmented customers into high, medium, and low value groups based on spending behavior

### 📊 Power BI Insights
- Built an interactive dashboard with KPIs: Total Spend, Average Spend, and Income Utilization %
- Visualized spending trends across categories, cities, age groups, and payment methods
- Identified monthly spending patterns to understand customer behavior over time
- Enabled dynamic filtering using slicers (City, Gender, Age Group) for better exploration
- Highlighted key segments contributing most to revenue

### 💼 Business Insights & Recommendations
- Customers show high income utilization (~119%), indicating strong potential for credit card adoption
- The 25–34 age group contributes the highest spending and is a key target segment
- Metro cities such as Mumbai and Bengaluru show higher spending activity
- Spending is concentrated in essential categories like Bills and Groceries
- Credit Cards and UPI are the most preferred payment methods

#### 📌 Recommendations:
- Target high-income utilization customers with premium and reward-based credit cards
- Focus on young professionals in metro cities for higher adoption rates
- Introduce cashback and benefits for high-spend categories
- Use customer segmentation to design personalized credit card offerings
